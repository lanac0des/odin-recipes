Seafood Miso Ramen

Description

Ramen is a hearty Japanese dish that can be prepared in many ways. This recipe will show you how to make miso ramen with seafood toppings. Feel free to substitute toppings as desired!

Ramen is comprised of three main parts: the broth, the noodles, and the toppings. For this particular ramen, the broth flavor is miso, the toppings are seafood and veggies, and the noodles are standard ramen style noodles. There are many different combinations of these three parts, and therefore many ways to prepare ramen. Ramen typically includes a hearty and healthy variety of vegetables, proteins, and nutrients, so this dish will fit right into a well-balanced diet.

The seafood miso ramen that you'll learn to make from this recipe will be rich in essential fatty acids, protein, an assortment of vitamins, minerals, antioxidants, and other benefits. Miso paste is an extremely flavorful soybean product, and pairs very well with a variety of broth bases such as chicken, vegetable, or beef broth. Miso paste comes in three main varieties: white (mildest flavor), yellow (moderate flavor), and red (most pungent flavor). This recipe will be using white miso paste. The ideal toppings for a bowl of ramen vary depending on the broth flavor and an individual's preferences. This makes ramen a fun and versatile meal option.

Ingredients

Main Ingredients

  • ramen style noodles
  • white miso paste
  • chicken bone broth
  • veggie broth
  • shrimp (preferably raw)
  • bay scallops
  • white mushrooms
  • imitation crab sticks
  • baby carrots
  • an egg

General Ingredients

  • butter
  • salt
  • black pepper
  • Old Bay seasoning
  • smoked paprika
  • olive oil

Cookware

  • one large pot
  • one large skillet
  • cutting boardand knife
  • one small pot or an egg cooker

Steps

  1. Begin by boiling the egg and unthawing the shrimp and scallops.
  2. Combine the chicken broth with the veggie broth in the large pot, and heat it slowly. When the broth starts to steam, stir in the miso paste.
  3. 5 minutes after the miso paste is added, turn the heat down to the lowest setting and add the noddles.
  4. Wash the carrots and mushrooms, then chop them into bite-sized chunks.
  5. Season the thawed seafood with old bay, salt, pepper, and smoked paprika.
  6. Put a tablespoon of butter and a capful of olive oil into the skillet and set the heat to low to melt the butter. After the butter melts, stir fry all the toppings together.
  7. Put the broth, noodles, stir fry, imitation crab strips, and the boiled egg (halved) into a bowl. Tear the imitation crab strips apart (like string cheese) before adding.
  8. Enjoy your meal!
